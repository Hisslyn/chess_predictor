**Context-Aware Strategy Recommendation in Swiss Chess Tournaments Using Machine Learning**
## Abstract
Outcomes in Swiss-system chess tournaments depend not only on per-game probabilities but on accumulating context — current standing, opponent strength so far, rounds remaining, and color allocation. The Elo rating system models single-game expectation but ignores this structure, and the strategic dimension of round-level play (whether to play aggressively, solidly, or passively) is rarely modeled at all. We address both gaps with a context-aware recommender. Using 66,594 player-round observations from 147 Swiss tournaments scraped from chess-results.com, we train an XGBoost regressor that predicts a player's final FIDE performance rating (Rp) conditioned on situational features and a candidate strategic action. On 29 held-out tournaments the model attains a mean absolute error of 94.3 Elo points, beating a rating-bracket-mean baseline by 73.8 points and an Elo expected-score baseline by 50.4 points — a 35% reduction against the stronger baseline; the cross-validated MAE of 88.5 indicates limited overfitting. SHAP analysis identifies score deviation from Elo expectation as the leading dynamic predictor; the action one-hots carry small but non-zero gain (0.0113–0.0146), with passive play associated with lower predicted Rp and aggressive play with higher Rp after controlling for player and opponent strength. The trained model is exposed as a per-round recommender that scores all three actions and surfaces the SHAP drivers behind its choice. The situation × action → predicted-outcome scaffold is a generic recommender pattern that could transfer to other sequential-decision domains, though task-specific labels and validation would be required.
